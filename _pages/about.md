PhD from IIT Guwahati specializing in 5G/6G and next-generation wireless communication.
My research harnesses AI/ML to pioneer advanced Channel Estimation and NOMA techniques for a more connected future.

## Research Interests



My research lies at the intersection of wireless communication and artificial intelligence, with a focus on developing innovative solutions for 5G-Advanced and 6G networks. My specific interests include:

* AI/ML for Wireless Physical Layer: Designing deep learning and data-driven algorithms for intelligent signal processing in complex wireless environments.

* Next-Generation Multiple Access: Advanced research on Non-Orthogonal Multiple Access (NOMA) and its integration with other 5G/6G technologies like massive MIMO.

* Intelligent Channel Estimation & CSI Acquisition: Developing low-overhead, high-accuracy channel state information (CSI) estimation techniques using model-based and data-driven AI methods.

* 6G Vision and Enabling Technologies: Exploring the fundamental building blocks of 6G, including semantic communications, integrated sensing and communication (ISAC), and reconfigurable intelligent surfaces (RIS).

* Resource Optimization in Wireless Networks: Applying machine learning and optimization theory to dynamically manage spectrum, power, and computational resources in dense and heterogeneous networks.
## Education

I hold a Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati, which follows my foundational graduate and undergraduate studies completed at the University of Calcutta, Kolkata.
